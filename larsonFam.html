˜Cyrus and Arvie Nelson˜

The Larson side of my family has some very interesting facts I've found so far. The Nelson family actually founded Nelson, Montana. Cyrus and Arvie had 7 children together and both lived to be in their 90s.

˜Emil and Mary Gerky˜

The Gerky family line came to the United Staes from Germany. The family lived in Iowa, North Dakota, and then moved to Montana. Emil and Mary Gerky had 7 children, their daughter Allena would later marry Theodore Larson in Havre, Montana.

˜Peder and Charlotte Jeppsen˜

Peder Jeppsen was born in Denmark where he met and married his wife Charlotte Nielsdotter Jeppsen. The couple had a large family of 15 children, unfortunately not all children had a long life. It's also possible he had 2 children from a different relationship. Peder and Charlotte traveled to the U.S. with the family in 1901 arriving in New York. From there they moved to Utah where 2 of their sons became a blacksmith and an electrician according to the 1910 Census.

˜Johan and Christine Larson˜

Johan Larson was born in Sweden in 1850 however I haven't been able to find any other family members. He arrived in New York in 1868, after settling in the U.S. Johan started going by John. John moved to Iowa to become a farmer and later married Christine Hanson in 1874 where the couple had 7 children.

˜Theodore and Allena Larson˜

Theodore Larson, of Johan and Christine Larson, was born 1882 in Iowa and would later marry Allena Gerky in 1909. The couple moved to Havre Montana where they owned their own farm and had 6 children together. Johan would pass the farm down to his son Earl and his family.

˜Earl and Martha Larson˜

Earl Larson was born to Theodore and Allena Larson in Havre 1917. At the age of 22 he married Martha Lena Kostecka, daughter of Josef Kostecka Jr. and Antonie "Nettie" Prokes of Czechoslovakia. Earl and Martha worked hard and through their life were able to grow their farm tremendously and eventually started ranching as well. They taught their three kids, Mavis Marie and Robert, the tricks of farming and ranching to eventually hand the farm down to their youngest, Robert, and his family. In their older years, Martha had a stroke which put her in a nursing home. Earl would visit her every day, nothing would stop him, from 12:30 to 5:00, Earl would always say she might come out of and would be able to come home.

˜Martha and Joseph Kostecka˜

Martha was born to Josef Kostecka Jr. and Antonie "Nettie" Prokes in Czechoslovakia. Both Josef and Antonie were born in Czechoslovakia until they came to the United States. As an adult Josef was in the carpentry business until arriving in the United States in 1908. Netting planned to leave for America to be with Joseph on April 10, 1912 on The Titanic but luck was in her favor that day. Nettie forgot a suitcase as the ship was boarding, she went back to grab her suitcase and didn't make it back in time to board the ship. She ended up arriving in the United States August 1912. Nettie and Josef married in Minnesota in 1914 where they had their first two children Marcella and Stanley. It was once the family moved to Havre to start their farm is when the family had their third child, Martha. This couple is one where I had been told Josef died years before Nettie but I found paperwork showing Nettie and Josef divorced in 1950 for "mental cruelty" and Josef died in California in 1968 while Nettie stayed in Havre until her death in 1988.

˜George and Dortha Neuberg˜

My grandmas parents were George Neuberg born 1913 in Ramsey, Minnesota to Gustav and Anna Neuberg from Denmark and Dortha Nelson born 1916 in Malta, Montana to Henry and Nellie Nelson. George and Dortha married in Granite, Montana 1941 the day after Valentines Day then settled in Butte, Montana where they had Georgia, Nancy, and Reno while George worked as a Foreman. Once their children had their own families the couple moved to Billings were they both died in 1997.

˜Bob and Georgia Larson˜

Georgia married Robert "Bob" Larson in Gallatin, Montana June 1968. They later had their first daughter, Laura, December 1968 in Bozeman, Montana. The couple moved to California for a short amount of time but decided they didn't like California and moved back to Bob's home town of Havre, Montana. Once settled in Havre the couple had their othe 2 daughters Nichole and Dominique. Bob helped his dad with farming and ranching while working for the city as an engineer and Georgia worked as a lab tech at the hospital. The couple now has 2 grandchildren from each daughter, Dominique and her husband Shawn took over the ranch, while Nichole and Laura both moved to Great Falls with their families.

˜Nichole Larson˜

Nichole met Jeff Newton while they were going to school at MSU, where Jeff played for the football team. Nichole Married Jeff 1993, they lived in Seattle for a few years while Jeff was working for the Seattle Police Department and Nichole was working as a teacher. Today they are in Great Falls where Nichole works as a speech therapist in the public school system and has her own business while Jeff works as the Cheif of Police in Great Falls. Their daughter Taylor is in her third year of college at MSU and their son Zach is a senior at Great Falls High who is hoping to follow in his dads' footsteps playing football as MSU next year.

˜Dominique Larson˜

Dominique went to Carrol College and became a pharmacist, where she met her husband Shawn. Shawn now runs the farm and is a mechanic while Dominique owns her own pharmacy in Havre. They have 2 boys, Koby and Stephen, who are both in high school. When the boys aren't helping out on the farm Koby is a thespian and a cross country running and Stephen is a football player looking forward to college next year.

˜Laura Larson˜

Laura went to the University of Montana to become a physical therapist where she met her husband Chris, who moved to Missoula from Lethbridge, Alberta for school. Their first few years of marriage they were floating around the state but eventually landed in Power, Montana where they had their daughter, Jordyn, July 1996. Shortly after that Chris went back to school to become a PA which is when they decided it would be best to move into Great Falls and then had their son, Corbin, December 1998. Laura is now the CEO of the Chester Hospital, but is planning to accept a job at Benefis soon, and Chris has been working for the same orthopedic surgeons the last 23 years as thier PA. Jordyn had a hard time deciding at first on what she wanted to do but is now on her third year working full time as a medical coder at the hospital and going to the local college as a full time student for computer programming and cybersecurity. Corbin graduated from MSU Bozeman last winter and has now started his first year of medical school through the WAMI Program in Bozeman where he hopes to become a surgeon but isn't sure of what specialty yet.
